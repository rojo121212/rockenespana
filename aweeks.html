  • being young in Sevilla

Day 02

Woke up this morning, did not sleep a lot (still managing my jet lag as I only got 4 hours of sleep). Got brunch and spoke with a french student stuyding english in Spain! Then, went to the Mercadillo hippie artesanal in Sevilla and bought a pretty cute pair of purple pants (that are not as baggy as I had wanted...) First Spanish Crunchwrap Supreme and Lime Fanta! I went back to do some work, get dinner, and then watched Culpa Mia with my peers. Lovely bonding time.

Day 03

Woke up around 12ish today for the final time- sleep schedule is still not cemented, as the movie hangout went until about 3:30 am. I've gone a day without taking a midday nap which is new, but still need to adjust to going to bed at a reasonable time. For brunch, on my plate I had eggs, chocolate covered mini donuts, ham, and a chocolate crossaint. The only thing I ended up finishing was the crossaint (who's surprised) because I found that the eggs are made of a texture I am not a fan of. I already don't love eggs alone, which is why I got the ham, but the eggs were somehow sweet and consisted of a more jelly like structure that did not sit right with me. The crossaint was good and I used the machine to make a cappucino to hopefully wake me up. Over brunch, we watched highlights from the OKC and Spurs game, while I shopped for some slides that would assist me in my knee recovery, as I feel that my nike sneakers do not provide the most ideal support to me at the moment. Going to the market today! Seeing if I can find a polo or maybe some linen pants :)
So. Did not end up getting either polo OR linen pants, but I did find some cool shorts and an athletic top suitable for bed here. Unfortunately, the markets were closed today, and I WILL need to go back at some point this week to exchange my pants for a new size. We stopped to get burgers and pizza on the way back to the residence, and I ended my night with a sunset swim as we met some new Spanish students.

Day 04

Today I woke up for my next day of orientation! A wonderful Monday morning, I went to breakfast with my peers and we headed to the Education Abroad Sevilla office. We went over one last presentation detailing our schedule for classes and activies outside of our internship, and discussed our expectations vs reality in our internship before thinking about what skills we could bring to each organization. One of my favorite parts of the day was our Drop-Off activity, one of SIT's best and oldest practices, where we scoured the city for a scavenger hunt-like exploration. Talking to locals about Sevilla traditions, finding an iconic landmark in Sevilla, and getting back in time.
After lunch at the residence, I took a nice long nap and went to the mall nearby after to look for some cool clothes or gifts to bring back to family at home. I got some popeyes (not very Spanish I know) and called my girlfriend to brag about my oreo ice cream. I got a shirt that said Espana! I watched Smallville with my peer, soon to be working at the same internship organization as me, Mujeres Supervientes! And called my lovely beautiful girlfriend. ❤️
